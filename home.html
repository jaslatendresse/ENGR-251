What is Thermodynamics?

Thermodynamics

thur-moh-dahy-nam-iks

Thermodynamics is the field of physics that explains the relationships between energy and a defined system. The system can be as small as a water drop or as big as the universe. This branch of science is more specifically concerned with heat, temperature, specific heat, entropy, pressure, enthalpy, etc...All of which are properties defining the said system. For many years, we have been interested in describing how a system interacts with its surroundings or how two bodies exchange energy whether it's by heat transfer or mechanical work.

While many of our daily life activites such as brewing a simple cup of coffee can be described with thermonadynamics, we will mainly focus on what is considered to be, even up to this day, the absolute truth: The First Law of Thermodynamics.

First Law of Thermodynamics

Consider a hot cup of freshly french pressed coffee left on a table. Assuming that the coffee and its environment are at different temperatures, the first law requires that the heat moved from the cup of coffee is the same as the heat added to its surroundings.

The first law of thermodynamics can be applies to two different types of system: a closed system and an open system. A closed system is also called a control mass because the mass of the system can never cross the boundaries; it remains constant at all times. An open system is said to be a control volume because while the mass can flow in an out, the volume remains constant at all times.
For further topics such as entropy, a third system called isolated system is being considered. In this case, no energy or mass can cross the boundaries. Therefore the system does not interact with its suroundings.
